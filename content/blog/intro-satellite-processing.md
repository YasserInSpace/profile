Satellite image processing is the foundation of Earth observation. Whether you are tracking deforestation, monitoring urban growth, or mapping geological features, understanding how to manipulate spectral bands is crucial.

### Spectral Bands Explained
Satellites like Sentinel-2 and Landsat 8 capture light across various wavelengths. The visible spectrum (Red, Green, Blue) is just a small part. 
- **Near-Infrared (NIR):** Highly reflective for healthy vegetation.
- **Shortwave-Infrared (SWIR):** Excellent for distinguishing moisture content in soil and vegetation.

### Preprocessing Steps
Before analyzing the data, we must correct for atmospheric interference. Techniques like Top-of-Atmosphere (TOA) and Bottom-of-Atmosphere (BOA) corrections ensure that the pixel values represent true surface reflectance.

By mastering these preprocessing techniques, we set a solid foundation for advanced machine learning classifications.
